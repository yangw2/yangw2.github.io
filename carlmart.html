Carlmart

Created by: Colin James, Khizar Qureshi, Sho Tanaka, and Wesley Yang.

Carlmart is designed to reduce waste by enabling people to buy and sell used items. It promotes sustainability by encouraging second-hand purchases and making affordable shopping accessible to everyone.

  • Search Function: Quickly find items users need.
  • Organized Main Page: Display available items with prices for easy browsing.
  • Eco-Friendly Shopping: Reduce waste and promote sustainability.

Final Three Requirements:

The Buy Page will serve as the main page of the website, allowing users to browse and purchase items. A Shopping Cart Page enables users to store items before purchasing instead of buying each item separately. The Sell Page allows users to upload items for sale, including images, descriptions, and contact details.

Addressing Requirements:

Our team implemented a Buy Page, Shopping Cart Page, and Sell Page to meet our final three requirements. The prototype includes key features like payment options for sellers and filter options for buyers.

UX Process:

We conducted fictional inquiry interviews to guide our design decisions. Team members interviewed different stakeholders and used two interviews to create a codebook and generate requirements.

Analysis Process:

Our analysis process involved axial coding, creating an affinity diagram, making a codebook, and generating requirements.

Interaction Flow:

  • Navigate from Buy Page to Sell Page via the profile button.
  • Navigate from Buy Page to Shopping Cart via the shopping cart button.
  • Open filters using the triangle button on the Buy Page.
  • Access Login Page via the “Sign in or Register” link.
