Carlmart

Step 1 - Carlmart Overview

Created by: Colin James, Khizar Qureshi, Sho Tanaka, and Wesley Yang.

Carlmart is designed to reduce waste by enabling people to buy and sell used items. It promotes sustainability by encouraging second-hand purchases and making affordable shopping accessible to everyone.

  • Search Function: Quickly find items users need.
  • Organized Main Page: Display available items with prices for easy browsing.
  • Eco-Friendly Shopping: Reduce waste and promote sustainability.

Final Three Requirements:

For our project, we decided that the final three requirements would be a Buy Page, Shopping Cart page, and Sell Page. The Buy Page will serve as the main page of the website, and enable users to browse and purchase items. Having a dedicated buy page will allow users to navigate our platform and browse items more easily. A Shopping Cart Page is important because it allows users to store a list of items they are interested in purchasing instead of having to buy each item separately. A Sell Page will allow users to upload items they are trying to sell to our website. This is where users can upload a picture and description of the item as well as their information.

Addressing Requirements:

The WACK team chose a buy page, shopping cart page, and sell page for our final three requirements. For my prototype I made a dedicated page for each of our three chosen requirements. This addresses our team's final three requirements because it has the corresponding pages that we chose. I included elements that I expected these pages to have, like payment type for the sell page, and a filter button for the buy page.

UX Process:

Our team focused on fictional inquiry interviews for our UX research process. We decided that fictional inquiry would allow us to best find the information we are looking for and design our website based on what stakeholders like. Each of our team members conducted a fictional inquiry interview with different stakeholders and we used the information from two of the interviews to create our codebook and final three requirements.

Analysis Process:

Our team's analysis process consisted of six steps. Initial axial coding, affinity diagram, creating a codebook, axial coding with our codebook, consensus building, and requirements generation. The axial coding consisted of analyzing transcripts from our interview. We split into two groups consisting of Colin and Wesley, and Sho and Khizar, created an affinity diagram based on themes we saw in the transcripts, and made a consensus on each of our codebooks.

Interaction Flow:

  • Navigate from Buy Page to Sell Page via the profile button.
  • Navigate from Buy Page to Shopping Cart via the shopping cart button.
  • Open filters using the triangle button on the Buy Page.
  • Access Login Page via the “Sign in or Register” link.

Shopping Cart Interaction – Demonstrates how users can add items to their shopping cart and proceed to checkout.

Buy Page Interaction – Shows how users can browse items, filter products, and view details before purchasing.

Sell Page Interaction – Walks through the process of listing an item for sale, including adding a description and uploading images.

Login Page Interaction – Displays the login and registration process for new and returning users.
